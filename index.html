Bio

Le Liang received the B.E. degree in information engineering from Southeast University, Nanjing, China, in 2012, the M.A.Sc degree in electrical engineering from the University of Victoria, BC, Canada, in 2015, and the Ph.D. degree in electrical and computer engineering from the Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, GA, USA in 2018. He is a Research Scientist with Intel Labs, Hillsboro, OR, USA. His general research interests include wireless communications, signal processing, and machine learning.

Dr. Liang serves as an Editor for the IEEE Communications Letters and as an Associate Editor for the IEEE Journal on Selected Areas in Communications Series on Machine Learning in Communications and Networks. He was the Industry Sponsorship Chair of the IEEE International Workshop on Signal Processing in Wireless Communications (SPAWC) in 2020 and the Chair of the IEEE SmartGridComm Workshop on Convergence of Vehicular Networks and Smart Grids in 2019. He received the Best Paper Award at the IEEE/CIC International Conference on Communication in China (ICCC) in 2014 and was named an Exemplary Reviewer for the IEEE Wireless Communications Letters in 2018.
